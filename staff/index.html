We will help you to get to the top of YOUR game!

Rick Mahorn

Big Man Skills Lead Coach

Rick Mahorn was a member of the legendary Detroit Pistons “Bad Boys,” a veteran of 18 season in the NBA as well as two seasons in Italy. Rick is currently the radio voice of the Pistons.

Click here to read about Rick and the Bad Boys, who reunited during half-time at a recent Pistons game.

“My goal will be to get you to always be a student of the game. Hakeem was one of the most gifted post players, but guys like Kareem and Kevin McHale, once they developed a counter-move became much harder to defend. You will leave the camp with a better understanding of the game, and of YOUR game that will help you become the best player you can be. Based on my many years as a player and coach, I would say that the MENTAL part of the game is holding far too many players back today. Combine the mental aspect with the physical skills and you will become a more complete player. THIS COMBINATION MAKES OUR CAMP THE MOST UNIQUE CAMP IN THE WORLD.”

Dr. Mitch Smith

Director of Mental Training

Mitch Smith has worked with hundreds of pro and college basketball players, coaches and teams around the U.S. and Europe for the past 20 years, assisting them to develop the mental focus and psychological qualities to achieve greater competitive success. In addition, he has helped dozens of international players to receive scholarships to play at colleges in the U.S. To read some of Mitch Smith articles discussing his work with players, Click Here.

"The true test of a camp is can it help you become a more dominating player IN IMPORTANT GAME SITUATIONS!!!  That is where the MENTAL part comes in. For 25 years I have helped players become MENTALLY TOUGHER so that they can:

  • Maintain constant focus and deal with distractions
  • Understand the key strategies to keep their confidence high
  • Use visualization to compete at the highest level
  • Set goals for constant improvement
  • Make anger their friend and not their enemy
  • Become more resilient and more aggressive"
